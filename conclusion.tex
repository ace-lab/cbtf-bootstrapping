\section{Conclusion}

\lesson{No unit at your institution ``owns'' exam
  administration.}

Delivering exams securely and efficiently does not involve
just the LMS, or just the firewall, or just the lab facilities, or just the
scheduling, or just the proctors; it requires coordination among all
these elements, responsibility for which is usually distributed across
various units on a campus.
% Nobody ``owns'' entire-course proctoring as a whole.
Centralizing responsibility for whole-course proctoring requires new
technical and social cooperation across non-porous organizational
boundaries.
Most challenges we faced inherit from this challenge in some
way.
Nonetheless, successfully confronting them has led to an
assessment-delivery model that is (as others before us have found)
\emph{far\/} superior to traditional exams in most respects.
The proof of the pudding is that the CBTF is now generating its own demand.

Our experience
demonstrates that the model pioneered at UIUC can be 
incrementally bootstrapped in an environment with different cultural
and administrative constraints without ``boiling the ocean.''  We are
confident that other institutions can do as we have done, and we
hope our lessons and reflections will help them succeed.
