\begin{abstract}

In Fall 2023, we ran a pilot of a Computer-Based Testing Facility
(CBTF) inspired by the extensive experience with CBTFs as pioneered at
the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign~\cite{zilles-testing-less-trying}.
Like that effort, our goal was to support proctored
computer-based testing
for entire courses, not just for students with special accommodations,
and to eventually serve the whole campus, not just one or two CS courses.
This paper describes our experience and lessons from
bootstrapping this effort, some of which became evident only in
hindsight as we scaled up to our current operation which is over
$20\times$ larger.
Our two main takeaways are as follows.
First, even in a small pilot, avoid adopting
  processes that won't scale.  Exceptional situations should create $O(1)$ work per
  student or per instructor rather than $O(n)$ work for CBTF staff.
Second, while various campus units typically ``own'' lab facilities, proctoring
  for students with disabilities, instructional software and hardware
  maintenance, room reservations, and so on, usually
  no one ``owns'' whole-course proctored exam administration.  You will have to create new
  administrative processes, disrupt existing ones, correct mistaken
  faculty assumptions, and coordinate across nonporous administrative
  boundaries.

\end{abstract}
